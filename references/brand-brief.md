# MallowMauve Brand Brief

## Positioning

- Luxury, minimal, and deeply rooted in Indian craftsmanship.
- Quiet, refined, and emotionally evocative rather than loud or commercial.
- Built around curated textile pieces, beginning with Kashmiri embroidered cushions.
- Meant to feel private and considered, not mass-market.

## Visual Direction

- Take cues from Aesop, The Row, and early-stage Sabyasachi digital presence.
- Favor generous whitespace, editorial pacing, and slow, intentional composition.
- Use soft, muted colors: ivory, mauve, beige, parchment, taupe, and charcoal.
- Keep contrast refined and warm instead of sharp or glossy.

## Typography

- Use an elegant serif for headings.
- Use a clean sans-serif for body copy and navigation.
- Keep line lengths calm and readable.
- Let typography create hierarchy instead of relying on many decorative elements.

## Layout Guidance

- Start with a centered or quietly placed wordmark and a restrained navigation.
- Build around a hero with atmosphere and room for copy to breathe.
- Present products as curated objects with minimal metadata.
- Include one section that speaks to craftsmanship and provenance.
- Close with a soft private-enquiry moment instead of a transactional checkout feeling.

## Copy Guidance

- Write short, poised sentences.
- Focus on materiality, handwork, mood, and placement in a home.
- Use language such as "curated", "quiet luxury", "hand-embroidered", "crafted in small editions", and "enquire privately".
- Avoid copy that sounds promotional, urgent, or mass retail.

## Anti-Patterns

- Bright accent colors.
- Cluttered navigation or crowded card layouts.
- Aggressive CTAs, discount language, or "shop now" energy.
- Popups, sliders, auto-rotating carousels, and badge-heavy interfaces.
- Generic stock imagery that breaks the quiet luxury mood.
